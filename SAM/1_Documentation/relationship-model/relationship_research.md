# Cross-Industry Research on Relationship Frameworks

*Compiled for SAM (relationship-coaching app design) — May 2026*

This document surveys how experts across six industries — organizational psychology, B2B sales, nonprofit fundraising, pastoral ministry, real estate, and relationship science — model the structure, growth, and decline of human relationships. Each section captures the expert's core model, definition of trust, indicators of health and decline, prescribed cadence, contact typology, and orientation toward transactional vs. covenantal relationships. A synthesis at the end identifies cross-industry convergences, divergences, and meaningfully different ontologies of "relationship."

Sources are linked inline. Where direct quotes appear, they are pulled from the cited page.

---

## 1. Patrick Lencioni (The Table Group) — Team Relationships & Organizational Health

Patrick Lencioni is the most influential popular writer on organizational team dynamics over the last twenty years. His work has three layers that interconnect: **The Five Dysfunctions of a Team** (interpersonal), **The Advantage / Four Disciplines** (organizational), and **The 6 Types of Working Genius** (individual + role-fit).

### Core model

**Five Dysfunctions Pyramid** — a layered model where each dysfunction enables the next ([BiteSize Learning](https://www.bitesizelearning.co.uk/resources/five-dysfunctions-of-a-team-summary-pyramid), [Table Group](https://www.tablegroup.com)):

1. **Absence of Trust** (foundation) — inability to be vulnerable with one another.
2. **Fear of Conflict** — teams that lack trust avoid productive ideological conflict.
3. **Lack of Commitment** — without honest debate, members don't truly buy into decisions.
4. **Avoidance of Accountability** — without commitment, peers won't call each other out.
5. **Inattention to Results** — without accountability, individuals prioritize ego and status.

**The Advantage / Four Disciplines of Organizational Health** ([Daniels College of Business](https://daniels.du.edu/blog/the-four-disciplines-of-organizational-health/), [Table Group](https://www.tablegroup.com/product/the-advantage/)):

1. Build a cohesive leadership team.
2. Create clarity (around 6 critical questions: Why do we exist? How do we behave? What do we do? How will we succeed? What's most important right now? Who must do what?).
3. Over-communicate clarity — "clearly, repeatedly and enthusiastically."
4. Reinforce clarity through systems (hiring, performance management, meetings, compensation).

**6 Types of Working Genius** ([workinggenius.com](https://www.workinggenius.com/), [Table Group](https://www.tablegroup.com/product/the-working-genius-book/)) — work passes through three stages (ideation → activation → implementation), with two "genius" types per stage: **W**onder, **I**nvention, **D**iscernment, **G**alvanizing, **E**nablement, **T**enacity. Each person has 2 geniuses (energizing), 2 competencies (tolerable), and 2 frustrations (draining).

### Trust

Lencioni's definition is unambiguous: **vulnerability-based trust**, not predictive trust. From the Table Group ([3 Ways Leaders Can Build Vulnerability-Based Trust](https://www.tablegroup.com/3-ways-leaders-can-build-vulnerability-based-trust/)): "a leader must set the stage for that trust by being genuinely vulnerable with his or her team members." Predictive trust ("I know how you'll behave") is necessary but insufficient; vulnerability-based trust is the willingness to say "I don't know," "I was wrong," "I need help" — without fear of being used against you ([Myers-Briggs interview](https://www.themyersbriggs.com/en-US/Access-Resources/Articles/lencioni-blog)).

How it's built (Lencioni's prescription): leaders go first. Share failure stories; celebrate employee superiority; allow employees to teach you.

### Indicators of health

- Honest, sometimes heated debate that ends in commitment.
- Members hold peers accountable without requiring the boss to mediate.
- "First-team" loyalty: members are more loyal to the leadership team than to their direct reports.
- Results take precedence over ego, status, or individual goals.

### Indicators of decline

- Politeness over candor; meetings are "boring."
- Same problems re-litigated because no one truly committed.
- Accountability is the boss's job alone.
- "Politics" — people optimize for personal/departmental wins.

### Cadence

Lencioni prescribes a **meeting cadence** as the heartbeat of organizational health: daily check-ins (5 min), weekly tactical (45–90 min), monthly strategic (2–4 hr), quarterly off-site review. He treats this rhythm as the operational expression of the four disciplines.

### Typology of contacts

Lencioni's typology is about **roles inside a team**, not external relationships:
- **Working Genius types** classify individuals by where they add energy.
- **The Ideal Team Player** model (humble, hungry, smart) classifies team members by character fit.

### Transactional vs. covenantal

Lencioni is firmly **covenantal** within the team. He explicitly contrasts the "transactional" team-as-collection-of-individuals model with the team-as-organism model where success is only possible through shared vulnerability and mutual accountability.

## 2. Sales / Business Development

I cover three complementary voices: **Neil Rackham** (research-grounded consultative selling), **David Sandler** (relationship-first transactional discipline), and **Matthew Dixon** (the Challenger counter-thesis).

### 2a. Neil Rackham — SPIN Selling

**Core model.** Rackham and Huthwaite studied 35,000+ sales calls and found high performers ask four types of questions in sequence ([readingraphics SPIN summary](https://readingraphics.com/book-summary-spin-selling-neil-rackham/), [Highspot](https://www.highspot.com/blog/spin-selling/)):

- **S**ituation — facts about the buyer's context (used sparingly).
- **P**roblem — surfaces dissatisfactions.
- **I**mplication — makes the cost of those problems vivid.
- **N**eed-payoff — gets the buyer to articulate the value of solving the problem.

**Trust.** Trust in SPIN is consultative and earned through evident competence and curiosity — the rep proves they understand the buyer's world before proposing anything. Rackham's signature line, quoted across the literature: *"The objective is not to close a sale, but to open a relationship."*

**Healthy signals.** Buyer articulates need-payoff in their own words (implicit needs become explicit). Buyer initiates follow-ups. Buyer surfaces additional stakeholders.

**Decline signals.** Buyer answers situation questions but won't engage with implication questions — they don't view the problem as costly enough.

### 2b. David Sandler — Sandler Selling System (Submarine)

**Core model.** Seven sequential compartments, grouped into three phases ([Avoma](https://www.avoma.com/blog/sandler-sales-methodology), [Salesforce](https://www.salesforce.com/blog/sales/sandler-sales-methodology/), [Salesmate](https://www.salesmate.io/blog/sandler-sales-methodology/)):

1. **Bonding & Rapport** — "being seen, heard, and understood."
2. **Up-Front Contract** — explicit agreement on what this conversation is for.
3. **Pain** — discovering the prospect's real motivation.
4. **Budget** — emotional + financial readiness.
5. **Decision** — who decides and how.
6. **Fulfillment** — present the solution.
7. **Post-Sell** — lock in commitment, prevent buyer's remorse.

**Trust.** Sandler treats trust as the prerequisite, not the outcome: *"buyers only open up when they feel respected and safe."* The "submarine" metaphor matters — you must **seal off each compartment** before progressing; skipping rapport contaminates everything downstream.

**Healthy signals.** Prospect reveals pain freely; agrees to mutual up-front contracts; volunteers budget and decision-process info.

**Decline signals.** Prospect won't agree to next-step contracts; vague about pain; "happy ears" on the seller's side (hearing yes when prospect is non-committal).

### 2c. Matthew Dixon & Brent Adamson — The Challenger Sale

**Core model.** Five rep profiles ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Challenger_Sale), [Teamgate](https://www.teamgate.com/blog/challenger-sales-model/)): **Hard Worker, Relationship Builder, Lone Wolf, Reactive Problem Solver, Challenger**. Challengers consistently outperform — they **teach, tailor, and take control**.

**The provocation.** Dixon and Adamson argue *"classic relationship building is a losing approach"* in complex B2B sales. Buyers don't want a friend; they want someone who reframes their problem with commercial insight.

**Trust.** Trust comes from *teaching the customer something they didn't know about their own business* — earned authority, not interpersonal warmth.

**Healthy signals.** Customer's mental model shifts during conversation; they describe the rep as someone who "challenges my thinking." Mobilizer-stakeholders (internal change agents) emerge.

**Decline signals.** Heavy reliance on relationship-only check-ins; the rep is the only one initiating; no internal Mobilizer; loss of executive engagement.

### Cross-sales decline signals (consolidated)

From [Hive Perform](https://hiveperform.com/resource-hub/the-hidden-early-signals?hs_amp=true), [Mixmax](https://www.mixmax.com/blog/when-to-stop-pursuing-deal), [Outreach](https://www.outreach.ai/resources/blog/warning-signs-your-deal-is-at-risk):
- Communication latency increasing (response time stretches).
- Every contact initiated by the seller.
- Champion goes silent or changes role.
- Soft language: "We're still reviewing," "Let's reconnect next month."
- CRM hygiene degrades (fewer notes logged) — a leading indicator on the seller's side.
- Statistic worth noting: *"71% of buyers who disengage during the sales process never reconnect."*

### Cadence

SPIN/Sandler don't prescribe fixed cadences — pacing is buyer-led. Challenger emphasizes **insight-driven touches** (always bring something new to the conversation) rather than calendar-driven touches. Modern sales tooling (Outreach, Salesloft) has codified "sequence" cadences (e.g., 8–12 touches over 14–21 days for prospecting) but those are tactical scripts layered onto these strategic models.

### Typology

- SPIN: implicit-need vs. explicit-need buyers.
- Sandler: prospects in pain vs. tire-kickers.
- Challenger: Talkers (friendly but unhelpful) vs. Mobilizers (internal change agents) — the typology is about **buyer-side stakeholders**, not customer relationships per se.

### Transactional vs. covenantal

Sales is openly transactional in outcome but the better methodologies (SPIN, Sandler) treat the *process* as covenantal — the rep's obligation is to the buyer's truth, not to closing. Challenger pushes back: real value is in **commercial teaching**, not friendship.

## 3. Nonprofit / Donor Cultivation

Penelope Burk and Tom Ahern are the dominant voices on donor-centric communication; the **moves-management cycle** is the universal operational frame.

### Core model: The Donor Cultivation Cycle

Five stages, treated as a cycle, not a funnel ([Blackbaud](https://www.blackbaud.com/industry-insights/glossary/donor-cultivation-cycle), [Neon One](https://neonone.com/resources/blog/donor-cultivation-cycle/), [CASE](https://www.case.org/7-donor-cultivation)):

1. **Identification** — building the prospect list.
2. **Qualification** — confirming capacity and inclination.
3. **Cultivation** — relationship-building leading toward the ask.
4. **Solicitation** — making the ask.
5. **Stewardship** — thanking, reporting impact, deepening the bond before the next cycle.

Critically: stewardship **loops back** into cultivation. The cycle is explicitly designed to be iterative, not terminal.

### Penelope Burk's Donor-Centered Fundraising

Burk's six years of Cygnus Research with hundreds of thousands of donors distilled to three non-negotiable donor needs ([Cygnus Research](https://cygresearch.com/donor-centered-philosophy/), [Penelope's blog](https://burksblog.com/)):

1. Prompt, meaningful **acknowledgment** of every gift.
2. Every gift assigned to a **specific program** (narrower than the org's mission).
3. **Measurable reporting** on what was accomplished with the *last* gift before being asked for another.

Burk's central claim is empirical: donors leave because they feel unseen and underinformed, not because they ran out of money.

### Tom Ahern on retention

Ahern's bluntest line ([@thattomahern](https://twitter.com/thattomahern/status/480833966707326977)): *"If you don't measure donor retention, your communications program is broken at the stem. Only one metric matters: Are we keeping them?"* Ahern emphasizes **Lifetime Value (LTV)**, not single-gift size, as the proper unit of measurement ([Petrus Development interview](https://www.petrusdevelopment.com/blog/donor-centric-fundraising-interview-with-tom-ahern)).

### Trust

In fundraising, trust is operationalized as **transparency about impact**. Donors trust the org when they can see what their money did. Trust degrades when communications shift from impact-reporting to need-pleading.

### Indicators of health

- Donor retention rate (industry benchmark ~45% for new donors, 60%+ for repeat).
- Time from gift to thank-you (Burk: 48 hours is the gold standard).
- Donor upgrade rate (movement up giving levels).
- Number of "meaningful contacts" per donor per year (not raw touches — meaningful ones; see [Fundraising Transformed](https://www.fundraisingtransformed.com/blog/major-gift-metrics-that-actually-drive-behavior)).

### Indicators of decline

- Lapsed donor count rising.
- Donor downgrades (giving level dropping).
- Gift officer's portfolio "stuck" — donors stalled in cultivation without moves.
- Communications are 100% solicitation, 0% stewardship reports.

### Cadence

Major gift officers are advised to manage **125–150 active prospects max** ([Kindsight](https://kindsight.io/resources/blog/prospect-management-best-practices/), [Gravyty](https://gravyty.com/blog/14-faqs-about-donor-portfolio-management/)) — more than that and donors fall through the cracks. The portfolio is conventionally split into four roughly equal groups: stewardship, near-term solicitation (12–18 months), cultivation (capacity but not yet ready), discovery.

### Typology of donors

Capacity × Inclination grid:
- **Identification** (unknown capacity/inclination).
- **Qualification** (capacity confirmed, inclination unknown).
- **Cultivation** (both confirmed; not yet ready).
- **Solicitation-ready**.
- **Stewardship** (active major donors).
- **Lapsed** (dormant >12–24 months).

### Transactional vs. covenantal

Donor cultivation is **explicitly covenantal**. The cycle is built around the assumption that the relationship outlasts any single gift. Burk's philosophy is that one-shot giving is a symptom of org failure, not donor flakiness.

## 4. Pastoral / Faith-Based Community Leadership

Three voices: **Eugene Peterson** (pastor-as-presence), **Henri Nouwen** (pastor-as-wounded-host), **Larry Osborne** (pastor-as-architect-of-belonging).

### Eugene Peterson — Pastoral Ministry as Long Obedience

Peterson's lifetime project was a polemic against the pastor-as-CEO. His pastoral vision is **unhurried, unprogrammatic, name-knowing presence** ([The Gospel Coalition](https://www.thegospelcoalition.org/article/eugene-peterson-pastoral-ministry-books/), [Peterson Center](https://petersoncenter.org/holy-presence-a-pastoral-vision/), [Lifeway Research](https://research.lifeway.com/2021/01/21/why-a-long-obedience-in-the-same-direction-is-necessary-in-ministry/)).

The widely cited story: when Peterson's church grew past the point where he could know every member's name, **he stepped down** rather than pastor in name only. His phrase "a long obedience in the same direction" (borrowed from Nietzsche) names his anti-quick-fix theology of relationship: meaningful pastoral relationships require *years*, not programs.

### Henri Nouwen — Wounded Healer & Hospitality

Nouwen's framing ([Henri Nouwen Society](https://www.henrinouwen.org/books/the-wounded-healer), [Christos Center](https://www.christoscenter.org/blog/ministry-in-contemporary-society-reflections-on-the-wounded-healer)):

- The minister is a **host, not a doctor or parent.** Care is not a procedure performed *on* someone; it's a hospitality offered *to* someone.
- The minister leads from acknowledged woundedness: *"In our own woundedness, we can become a source of life for others."*
- Hospitality is the central practice: *"Hospitality is the virtue that allows us to break through the narrowness of our own fears and to open our house to the stranger."*

### Larry Osborne — Sticky Church

Osborne's contribution is structural: sermon-based small groups as the mechanism by which a large church becomes relationally sticky ([Christianbook](https://www.christianbook.com/sticky-church-larry-osborne/9780310285083/pd/285083), [Christian Post interview](https://www.christianpost.com/news/interview-larry-osborne-on-making-churches-sticky.html)).

- **Core thesis:** "Closing the back door is more important than opening the front door wider."
- **Mechanism:** Long-term Christian friendships keep people. Programs don't. Information doesn't.
- **Evidence:** At Osborne's North Coast Church, 80%+ of members have been in small groups for 20+ years.

### Trust

In pastoral models, trust is **fiduciary and covenantal** — the pastor holds people's spiritual welfare in trust. It's built through presence, confidentiality, and (Nouwen) by being unguarded about one's own brokenness. It's destroyed by: hypocrisy, broken confidence, and what Peterson calls "ego-driven ministry" (using people for the pastor's reputation).

### Indicators of health

- The pastor knows people's names, stories, life events.
- Members are in deep, long-running small groups.
- Pastoral care is distributed (elders, deacons, group leaders), not bottlenecked at the lead pastor.
- Members reach out *to* the pastor (and to each other) during life crises — they expect to be welcomed.

### Indicators of decline / neglect

- The "back door" is wide open — quiet departures without anyone noticing for months.
- Pastoral attention concentrates on the visible and demonstrative; the quiet middle is unseen ([Liberty University dissertation](https://digitalcommons.liberty.edu/context/doctoral/article/1232/viewcontent/Rummage_Aaron.pdf)).
- Pastor-as-performer rather than pastor-as-presence.
- Life events (births, deaths, hospitalizations, marriages) go unmarked by the church.

### Cadence

Pastoral models resist explicit cadence prescriptions — they're suspicious of the very idea. But operationally: weekly worship, weekly small group, marked life events, regular home visits (in older models). The discipleship rhythm is **liturgical** (year-round, seasonal) rather than campaign-driven.

### Typology of contacts

- Members vs. attenders vs. seekers vs. lapsed.
- Within members: in-group vs. uncared-for (the dissertation literature names this gap directly).

### Transactional vs. covenantal

This domain is **the** archetype of covenantal relationship. The whole vocabulary — shepherd, flock, family, body — denies the transactional framing. Pastoral "neglect" is the worst possible failure precisely because the relationship is supposed to be unconditional.

## 5. Real Estate / Referral-Based Personal Business

The real-estate coaches have built the most operationally precise relationship-cadence frameworks of any industry surveyed. Two foundational voices: **Brian Buffini** (Work by Referral) and **Gary Keller** (Millionaire Real Estate Agent).

### Brian Buffini — Work by Referral

Buffini's premise: every person has ~200 people in their sphere of influence; systematically nurtured, this sphere produces a predictable referral engine ([Buffini blog](https://blog.buffini.com/expand-your-sphere-of-influence/), [Work by Referral PDF](https://downloads.buffiniandcompany.com/documents/resources/WorkByReferral_2015_Color.pdf)).

**Five Fundamentals** ([blog.buffini.com](https://blog.buffini.com/5-fundamentals-working-referral/)):

1. **Give it out in slices and it comes back in loaves** — generosity before asking.
2. **Commit to a system** — consistency over inspiration.
3. **Have goals** — measurable targets.
4. **Stay in touch** — personal contact (not just digital).
5. **Express gratitude** — notes, calls, gestures.

The operational primitives: **calls, notes, Pop-Bys** (in-person drop-ins). *"The more you consistently stay in touch, the more your clients rely on you."*

### Gary Keller — Millionaire Real Estate Agent (12 Direct / 33 Touch)

Keller's segmentation is the cleanest in any industry surveyed ([Osprey CRM](https://ospreycrm.com/gary-kellers-lead-referral-programs-the-nuts-and-bolts/), [Chicago Realtor](https://chicagorealtor.com/stay-top-mind-33-touch-plan/), [Rev Real Estate School](https://www.revrealestateschool.com/tips/33-touch-campaign), [Shortform](https://www.shortform.com/blog/the-millionaire-real-estate-agent-gary-keller/)):

- **"Haven't Met" database** → **12 Direct plan**: 12 direct-mail pieces per year. Expected yield: 1 sale per 50 contacts annually.
- **"Met" database** → **33 Touch plan**: 33 contacts per year (mailers, calls, notes, events). Expected yield: 2 sales per 12 contacts annually — typically 1 repeat + 1 referral.

The numbers matter because they convert relationship cadence into expected business outcomes — a rare quantification in this literature.

### Tom Ferry — Database segmentation

Ferry's emphasis is on **segmenting the database by intent and interest**, not just relationship strength ([Tom Ferry blog](https://www.tomferry.com/blog/practical-real-estate-marketing-guide/), [Revaluate case study](https://blog.revaluate.com/tom-ferry-and-revaluate-case-study-2-5b-in-lost-listings-found-in-real-estate-lead-gen-database/)). His case study found agents leave billions on the table by failing to mine their existing database.

### A/B/C contact segmentation (industry convention)

Across multiple sources ([Propphy](https://www.propphy.com/blog/real-estate-sphere-of-influence-soi-definition-examples-90-day-plan), [Rev Real Estate School](https://www.revrealestateschool.com/tips/ultimate-soi-guide)):

- **A contacts**: Monthly personal contact. Top advocates, past clients who refer.
- **B contacts**: Monthly email + quarterly personal. Warm SOI.
- **C contacts**: Quarterly email + social. Aware but cool.

### Trust

Real-estate trust is built through **demonstrated reliability and generosity**. Buffini's "slices before loaves" captures it: the agent must give value (market reports, vendor referrals, neighborhood info) repeatedly *before* and *after* a transaction — not just during.

### Indicators of health

- Repeat + referral business as % of total transactions. Industry benchmark cited: 82% of transactions come from referral/repeat ([Propphy](https://www.propphy.com/blog/real-estate-sphere-of-influence-soi-definition-examples-90-day-plan)); strong agents aim for 70–85% of closings from SOI.
- Number of past clients giving second or third referrals.
- Inbound contact from SOI (clients reaching out, not just being reached).

### Indicators of decline

- Past clients hire a different agent for their next move (the cardinal sin).
- "Cold" sections of the database — contacts not touched in 90+ days.
- Agent prospects strangers while past clients sit dormant.

### Cadence

This is where real-estate frameworks dominate. Specific, calendared, quantified:
- Daily: 5–10 purposeful SOI touches.
- Weekly: 2–3 meaningful conversations, 1 appointment set.
- Monthly: 1 event/education moment, 1–3 new reviews, 1 new referral source added.
- Annual: 33 touches per A contact, 12 mailers per C contact.

### Typology

A / B / C (Buffini & convention), Past Client / Top Advocates / Warm SOI / Vendor Partners (modern segmentation), Met / Haven't Met (Keller).

### Transactional vs. covenantal

Aspirationally covenantal, structurally transactional. The Buffini/Keller worldview is that the transaction is just one moment in a multi-decade relationship, and the agent who treats it as terminal loses the lifetime value. But the *measurement* is purely transactional: GCI, referrals-per-contact, sales-per-touch.

## 6. Marriage / Personal Relationships — John Gottman

Gottman is the most empirical relationship-science source in this survey: 50+ years of laboratory observation, ~93% predictive accuracy for divorce within six years ([Gottman Institute](https://www.gottman.com), [empathi.com](https://empathi.com/blog/what-is-the-gottman-four-horsemen/)).

### Core model: The Sound Relationship House

Seven levels ([Intuitive Healing NYC](https://www.intuitivehealingnyc.com/blog/2023/2/22/good-bones-gottmans-sound-relationship-house), [Rod Hetzel PhD](https://rodhetzelphd.com/wp-content/uploads/tgi-sound-relationship-house-updated.pdf)):

1. **Build Love Maps** — know your partner's inner world.
2. **Share Fondness & Admiration** — actively express appreciation.
3. **Turn Toward** instead of away (bids for connection).
4. **The Positive Perspective** — give the benefit of the doubt.
5. **Manage Conflict** — regulated, not solved.
6. **Make Life Dreams Come True** — support each other's aspirations.
7. **Create Shared Meaning** — rituals, narratives, values.

Two **load-bearing walls** flank the house: **Trust** and **Commitment**.

### Trust

Gottman defines trust behaviorally and statistically: it's the accumulated record of **turning toward bids**. In a six-year follow-up of newlyweds, couples who stayed married turned toward bids **86% of the time** in lab observation; couples who divorced averaged **33%** ([gottman.com — emotional bank account](https://www.gottman.com/blog/invest-relationship-emotional-bank-account/)).

A **bid** is "any attempt, large or small, to make emotional contact" — a question, a touch, a comment about the weather. Each turning-toward is a deposit in the **Emotional Bank Account**.

### Indicators of health

- **The Magic Ratio: 5:1** during conflict; **20:1** outside of conflict ([gottman.com magic ratio](https://www.gottman.com/blog/the-magic-ratio-the-key-to-relationship-satisfaction/)).
- Updated Love Maps — each partner can name the other's current stressors, hopes, friends, fears.
- High turn-toward rate on bids.
- Repair attempts during conflict are accepted (de-escalation works).

### Indicators of decline — The Four Horsemen

Listed in order of severity ([gottman.com four horsemen](https://www.gottman.com/blog/the-four-horsemen-recognizing-criticism-contempt-defensiveness-and-stonewalling/)):

1. **Criticism** — attacking character ("you always…") rather than complaining about behavior.
2. **Contempt** — the single strongest predictor of divorce. Sarcasm, mockery, name-calling, eye-rolling.
3. **Defensiveness** — playing innocent victim instead of taking responsibility.
4. **Stonewalling** — withdrawing, shutting down, emotional flooding.

These tend to appear as a **cascade**: criticism opens the door, contempt follows, defensiveness responds, stonewalling closes the loop.

### Cadence

Gottman's prescriptions are micro-cadenced — daily rather than weekly:
- Daily "stress-reducing conversation" (20 min).
- Weekly "State of the Union" meeting.
- Ongoing Love Map updates.
- 6-second kiss; explicit affection rituals.

Cadence here is **continuous attention**, not periodic outreach.

### Typology

Gottman's typology is about **relationship type** rather than partner type: **Masters** (couples whose ratios and behaviors predict long-term success) vs. **Disasters**. Within disasters, distinct subtypes by predominant horseman.

### Transactional vs. covenantal

Marriage is the paradigmatic covenantal relationship in this survey. Gottman's empiricism doesn't undermine that — it operationalizes it. The Emotional Bank Account metaphor is explicitly *not* transactional accounting; it's an account that compounds over decades and that catastrophic withdrawals (contempt) can drain disproportionately.

## 7. Bonus: Brené Brown — BRAVING

Brené Brown's contribution is a **decomposition of trust into observable behaviors**, useful as a diagnostic checklist when "trust" feels too monolithic to discuss.

**BRAVING** ([Brené Brown — Anatomy of Trust](https://brenebrown.com/podcast/the-anatomy-of-trust/), [BRAVING PDF](https://211sandiego.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/The-Anatomy-of-Trust-BRAVING.pdf)):

- **B**oundaries — clarity about what's okay and not okay.
- **R**eliability — doing what you say, repeatedly, over time.
- **A**ccountability — owning mistakes, making amends.
- **V**ault — confidentiality; not sharing what isn't yours to share.
- **I**ntegrity — choosing courage over comfort; what's right over what's easy.
- **N**onjudgment — both parties can ask for help without being judged.
- **G**enerosity — assuming the most generous interpretation of others' words and actions.

Brown frames trust as built through **"marbles in a jar"** — small, consistent moments of trustworthy behavior — not grand gestures. This dovetails directly with Gottman's bid-turning data.

## Synthesis

### Cross-industry convergences (agreed by >3 sources)

1. **Trust accrues through small, repeated behaviors — not grand gestures.** Gottman's bids, Buffini's calls/notes/Pop-Bys, Brown's marbles, Burk's prompt thank-yous, Lencioni's vulnerability moments — all describe trust as cumulative and behavior-based. **Implication for SAM:** track interaction *frequency and quality* over time, not single high-stakes events. A "marble jar" health score per contact is a defensible model.

2. **Communication latency is a leading indicator of decline — everywhere.** Sales (response time stretching), pastoral (back-door departures), donor (lapsed donors), real estate (cold database segments), marriage (turn-away rate). Across industries, the gap between *expected* and *actual* communication cadence is the first measurable signal that a relationship is decaying. **Implication for SAM:** velocity decay (relative to that person's baseline cadence) is a stronger signal than absolute time-since-contact.

3. **Initiation asymmetry signals decline.** Sales (every email seller-initiated), real estate (past clients hiring someone else), marriage (no bids being made), donor (donor never reaches in unsolicited). When *only one party* drives the relationship, it's already dying. **Implication for SAM:** track who initiates each interaction. A "directional ratio" per relationship is diagnostically powerful.

4. **The cycle, not the funnel, is the right shape.** Donor cultivation explicitly loops stewardship back to cultivation; real estate's referral-engine model is cyclical; pastoral discipleship is liturgical/seasonal; Gottman's house is built and maintained, not crossed. Only sales (and SAM's WFG pipeline) is genuinely linear — and even there, the better methodologies frame the *first* sale as the start of a multi-year relationship. **Implication for SAM:** model the Client stage not as a terminus but as the gateway to a stewardship loop (referrals, repeat business, recruiting).

5. **Naming the person matters.** Peterson knew each member's name. Burk requires gifts be tied to a specific program impact. Buffini's database personalizes every touch. Gottman's Love Maps are about specific knowledge of one partner. Across industries, **specificity is the enemy of neglect.** Generic touches don't build relationship — they signal that the contact is one of many. **Implication for SAM:** every coaching suggestion must name specifics — this person, this concern, this last conversation. Generic outreach drafts are anti-pattern.

6. **Leaders/initiators must go first on vulnerability.** Lencioni (leader vulnerability), Nouwen (wounded healer), Brown (the trust-builder discloses first), Sandler (rep models openness in up-front contracts). The party with more positional power has to be more transparent about their fallibility for trust to compound.

### Meaningful divergences

1. **Friendship vs. authority as the basis of trust.** SPIN/Sandler/Buffini/pastoral models build trust through warmth, presence, and reliability. Challenger/Lencioni-Working-Genius/major-gift fundraising build trust through *competence* — earned authority. The divergence reveals: **trust has two distinct currencies, and the right currency depends on what the other person needs.** Buyers want competence when stakes are high; donors want both impact-competence and warmth; congregants want presence and integrity; spouses want turning-toward. **SAM implication:** the recommended communication style for a given contact should depend on what kind of trust the relationship is built on. Coaching a Lead may be Challenger-mode; coaching an existing Client may be Buffini-mode.

2. **Cadence: prescriptive vs. responsive.** Real-estate and donor frameworks are deeply prescriptive (33 touches/year, 48-hr thank-yous, 125-prospect portfolios). Pastoral and sales frameworks are deliberately *non*-prescriptive — Peterson would have called 33 Touch dehumanizing. The divergence reveals two ontologies of relationship: **performative ("if I do these things, the relationship will work") vs. responsive ("the relationship reveals what's needed").** SAM probably needs to support both, but should default to prescriptive cadences for newer relationships and become more responsive as the system learns each person's actual rhythm.

3. **What counts as a "relationship."** Challenger says relationship-building in B2B is a *losing* strategy — value-creation is the relationship. Nouwen says care *is* the relationship. The divergence reveals that "relationship" can mean: (a) a record of useful exchanges, (b) a record of vulnerable moments, (c) a record of mutual aid, or (d) ongoing presence. **SAM implication:** the same contact list may contain relationships of all four types, and the coaching engine should adapt its frame per relationship — not force every contact into one model.

### Fundamentally different ontologies

Three distinct ontological framings emerged, not just terminology differences:

- **Relationship-as-portfolio (real estate, fundraising, sales).** People are positioned in a finite pipeline; capacity is bounded (125 donors, 200-person SOI, named pipeline stages); the operative verb is *manage*. Decline is measured against pipeline norms.

- **Relationship-as-organism (Lencioni, Gottman, pastoral).** The relationship is a living thing with health states. Decline is pathology. Cadence is metabolism. The operative verb is *tend*.

- **Relationship-as-covenant (pastoral, marriage, Burk).** The relationship is a *commitment* that pre-exists any particular interaction and persists through silence. Cadence is fidelity, not maintenance. The operative verb is *abide*.

WFG financial-strategist relationships actually traverse all three: a Lead is portfolio (manage), a Client is organism (tend), a long-time client/agent/spouse-in-the-business is covenant (abide). SAM's coaching engine should not collapse these into a single model. A unified scoring approach risks treating a covenantal relationship as a stalled deal — a category error that the user would feel as deeply wrong.
