# SAM Onboarding Showcase

## Introduction

SAM believes your first experience should feel guided, not overwhelming. It walks you through setup one step at a time, then continues teaching as you explore — surfacing help exactly when and where you need it.

*Suggested screenshot: getting-started/images/gs-01.png (Today view — the destination after onboarding)*

## First Launch Experience

When you open SAM for the first time, a focused onboarding flow guides you through setup. Each step explains what SAM needs and why.

SAM doesn't just take over your life. SAM asks for permission to access your Contacts, even asking you to specify which sub-group of contacts that SAM may manage. Calendar and Mail permissions follow, where you select your work accounts. Your personal accounts stay private. Communications permission covers iMessage, Phone, and FaceTime history. Microphone permission enables on-device dictation. Notifications lets SAM alert you to time-critical messages.

Every permission is explained plainly. Skip something now, enable it later from Settings.

## Security on First Launch

SAM is obsessed with security. If someone is using your computer, SAM hides your work data. To view data that SAM has collected, SAM requires user authentication: Touch ID or your system password, no exceptions. You can configure an idle timeout so SAM re-locks when you step away. A quick Touch ID tap gets you back in, but no-one else.

## Tooltip System

As you first explore SAM, unmistakable contextual tips appear. The tip "Your Top Priority" introduces the hero coaching card. "Your Action Queue" explains the outcome queue. "Daily Briefing" walks you through your morning briefing. "Your Relationships" describes health indicators. "Business Intelligence" introduces the dashboard. "Clipboard Capture" teaches the Control-Shift-V hotkey. "Quick Commands" reveals the Command-K palette.

Each tip includes a "Learn more" link to the relevant guide article. Tips appear once and then get out of your way. But you can re-enable them from Settings or toggle them all off entirely.

## Built-in Guide

SAM includes a searchable guide with seven sections and thirty-four articles. Getting Started covers dictation, clipboard capture, and privacy. Today and Coaching explains your briefing and outcome queue. People and Relationships covers contacts, notes, and the relationship graph. Business Dashboard walks through goals, pipelines, production, and strategic insights. Grow and Content covers lead acquisition and content drafts. Events and Presentations provides eleven articles on the full event lifecycle. Search explains universal search.

Type a keyword and results filter instantly.

## Adaptive Learning

You learn from SAM, but SAM learns from you. It tracks which suggestions you act on, dismiss, skip, rate, or mute. Over time, it adjusts what it recommends and how often. If you consistently act on follow-up reminders but dismiss content suggestions, SAM shifts priority accordingly. Visit "What SAM Has Learned" in Settings to see your preference profile. The result: a coaching assistant that feels more useful every week.
