# Social Imports

Your LinkedIn connections, Facebook friends, and Substack subscribers represent years of relationship-building — but that data is trapped in platforms that don't talk to each other or to most business tools.

SAM lets you import your social data and turn it into actionable relationship intelligence. SAM helps you export your LinkedIn data so that you can let SAM scan it to gain insights with respect to your connections, their job titles, companies, and even your message history. This saves you so much time! SAM will combine this information with your other information in order to help you quickly identify who in your network might be a great prospect, referral source, or recruit.

Facebook imports work the same way — friends, messages, and dates you connected. Substack imports bring in your subscriber list and your published posts. SAM even extracts your writing voice from your content so it can help you draft future posts that sound like you.

Remember: with each import SAM automatically matches the new information against your existing contacts. When it finds a match, it creates an enrichment candidate — a suggested update with the person's latest job title, company, or LinkedIn URL — that you review and approve before anything changes. New connections can be added to your SAM contacts group with one click.

Every interaction from your social platforms gets recorded as a touch event, giving SAM a fuller picture of your relationship history. And all of this processing happens entirely on your Mac — your social data is analyzed and the raw exports are discarded. SAM only keeps summaries and information important for your relationships.

The result: your scattered social networks become part of a unified, searchable relationship tool, not a disorganized tangle of raw information.
