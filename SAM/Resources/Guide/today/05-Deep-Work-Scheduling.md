# Deep Work Scheduling

When SAM recommends focused time for a task — proposal writing, meeting preparation, strategy planning — you can schedule a calendar time block directly from the outcome card.

## How It Works

1. SAM generates a coaching outcome that benefits from dedicated focus time
2. The outcome's action button shows **Schedule** instead of the usual action
3. Click **Schedule** to open the Deep Work Schedule sheet

## The Schedule Sheet

The sheet shows:

| Field | What It Does |
|-------|-------------|
| **Task title** | The outcome title — what you'll be working on |
| **Rationale** | Why this task matters (from the coaching outcome) |
| **Person** | The person this work relates to (if applicable) |
| **Duration** | Choose 30 minutes, 1 hour, 2 hours, or 3 hours — SAM suggests a duration based on the task type |
| **Date and time** | Pick when to schedule the block — defaults to the next available hour |

## What Gets Created

When you click **Schedule**, SAM creates a calendar event on the "SAM Tasks" calendar with:

- The task title as the event name
- The rationale in the event notes
- A link back to the related person (if applicable)
- The duration you selected

The event appears on your calendar like any other meeting, blocking that time for focused work.

## When Deep Work Is Suggested

SAM suggests deep work blocks for tasks that benefit from uninterrupted focus:

- **Proposal writing** — drafting a financial plan or recommendation
- **Meeting preparation** — reviewing relationship history and preparing talking points before an important meeting
- **Strategy sessions** — reviewing pipeline health or goal progress
- **Content creation** — writing a newsletter or social media content series
- **Administrative tasks** — compliance reviews, documentation updates

## Tips

- Accept the suggested duration as a starting point — you can always adjust it
- Schedule deep work during your peak hours (SAM knows when you're most productive from your calibration data)
- The calendar block helps protect your focus time from meeting requests
