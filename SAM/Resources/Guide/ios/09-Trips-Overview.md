# Mileage Tracking on iPhone

The Trips tab is SAM Field's IRS-compliant mileage tracker. Start a trip when you leave, tag stops as you go, and confirm trips at the end of the day so the log is contemporaneous.

## How to Use

1. Tap the **Trips** tab.
2. Tap **Start Trip** (top of the list when no trip is active). Location permission is required.
3. As you drive, SAM tracks the route and detects stops automatically.
4. Use **Add Stop** to manually log a visit with a purpose, business name, and notes.
5. Tap **Stop** to end the trip — or **Close at Home** if your home address is set (see "Closing a Trip at Home").
6. Review the trip summary, set the vehicle and purpose, then tap **Confirm Trip** to satisfy the IRS contemporaneous record requirement.

## What You See When Idle

- **Period filter** — Day, Week, Month, Year, All. Default is Month.
- **Period totals** — total miles and business miles below the filter.
- **Trip history** — most recent first (or grouped by month for Year/All views).
- Tap a trip row to view its summary; **swipe left** to delete.

## What You See During Tracking

- **Live map** with the route drawn in blue and stops as colored pins.
- **Distance** in miles, **stops** count, and tracking/paused status.
- **Add Stop**, **Pause/Resume**, **Stop**, and (if home address is set) **Close at Home** controls.

## Tips

- The **Confirm Trip** action is what makes the log IRS-compliant — unconfirmed trips show a banner on the Today tab to remind you.
- Add Stop pre-fills the **closest contact** when nearby contacts are configured (see Trip Settings → nearby-contact radius).
- Commuting trips (home ↔ regular office) are not tax-deductible — toggle **Commuting Trip** in the summary so they're excluded from your business miles.
- Use the **+ button** in the toolbar for **manual trip entry** when you forgot to start tracking.
