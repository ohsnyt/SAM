# Exporting Mileage for Taxes

At tax time (or whenever your accountant asks), the Trips tab can export your business mileage as a CSV or PDF that satisfies IRS recordkeeping requirements.

## How to Use

1. On the **Trips** tab (idle, no active trip), tap the **share icon** in the top-right.
2. Pick a **Date Range**:
   - **Tax Year** — current year, prior year, or two years back.
   - **Custom Range** — pick exact From/To dates.
3. Set the **IRS Rate per mile** (defaults to 0.70; update if the rate changed for the year).
4. Review the **Summary**: trip count, business miles, and estimated deduction.
5. Pick a **Format** — CSV or PDF.
6. Tap **Export** to generate the file, then share it via Mail, Messages, Files, or AirDrop using the system share sheet.

## What's Included

- One row per trip with date, vehicle, purpose, total miles, and business miles
- Start and end addresses, list of stops with purposes
- Confirmation timestamp (proof of contemporaneous record)
- Year totals and estimated deduction at the IRS rate

## What's Excluded

- Trips marked **Commuting Trip** are listed but flagged as non-deductible
- Personal stops within otherwise-business trips
- **Unconfirmed trips** are still included, but flagged — confirm them before sending to your accountant

## Tips

- Update the **IRS Rate** each January when the new standard rate is published.
- The PDF is human-readable; the CSV is best for importing into accounting software.
- Always export from the **Trips** tab, not Today — the share sheet only appears on Trips.
- Files are generated on-device; nothing is uploaded.
