# Setting Participants Before Recording

Diarization (telling speakers apart in the transcript) is much more accurate when SAM knows up front how many people are in the room and what their names are. The participant list on the Record tab is where you set that up.

## How to Use

1. On the Record tab, look at the **participant list** below the meeting title or context picker.
2. If a calendar event matched, names are pre-filled from the event's attendees — confirm or edit them.
3. To **add a participant**, tap **Add participant…** below the list (or use the + button in the stepper).
4. To **remove**, swipe left on a row. The list always keeps at least one participant.
5. To **rename**, tap the row and edit the text field.
6. Tap **Start Recording** — the speaker count and names are sent to the Mac as session metadata.

## How Names Are Pre-Filled

- If you have a calendar event matching the upcoming meeting (within the next 30 minutes or so), SAM uses its attendee list.
- Contact thumbnails appear next to attendees that match Apple Contacts.
- Otherwise the defaults are "You (Agent)" and "Client" — edit them as needed.
- For solo Training/Lecture recordings, the participant list is hidden — no setup needed.

## Tips

- **Use real names**, not roles like "Husband" or "Friend." The Mac's summary refers to people by what's in the participant list.
- Up to **8 speakers** are supported. More than that and diarization quality drops fast — break the recording into segments.
- You can edit participants again from the **Paused** state mid-recording (tap **Edit Participants**) if someone joins late.
- Calendar-matched events show their event title — that's how the Mac links the transcript back to the event afterward.
