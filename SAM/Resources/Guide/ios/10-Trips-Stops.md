# Automatic Stop Detection

While a trip is active, SAM watches for periods of low motion and creates a stop automatically when it looks like you've parked. You can review, rename, or assign a purpose to each detected stop.

## How It Works

- SAM monitors location and speed in the background.
- When you stay roughly stationary long enough to suggest a parked vehicle, a new stop is added to the trip.
- The stop is labeled with its address (reverse-geocoded) and a default purpose; you can change either.
- Each stop appears as a **pin on the map** and a **chip in the horizontal stop list** below the distance.

## How to Use

1. While tracking is active, watch the stops chip row scroll in as new stops appear.
2. Tap a chip to review the stop's purpose and outcome (after the trip ends, in the trip summary).
3. To **add a stop manually** (e.g., a quick errand the auto-detector missed), tap **Add Stop**, choose a purpose, and optionally name it.
4. After the trip ends, the **Stops** section in the summary lets you adjust each stop's purpose and notes.

## Stop Purposes

Available purposes include Client Meeting, Prospecting, Training, Office, Errand, Personal, and Commuting. Business purposes count toward business miles; personal ones don't.

## Tips

- Auto-detected stops are a starting point — review them before confirming the trip so the IRS log is accurate.
- If a stop is wrong (e.g., a long traffic light), delete it from the trip summary's Stops section.
- The **closest contact** is pre-filled in Add Stop when you're near someone in your contacts (configure radius in Trip Settings).
- Stop outcomes (e.g., "Met," "No Answer") help SAM coach you on follow-ups later — set them when you can.
