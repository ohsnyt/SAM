# Client Pipeline

Track your client funnel from Lead to Applicant to Client. The pipeline dashboard shows conversion rates, velocity, stuck contacts, and recent transitions.

## Pipeline Stages

Your client pipeline has three stages:

| Stage | Meaning | Badge Color |
|-------|---------|------------|
| **Lead** | Someone you've identified as a potential client | Orange |
| **Applicant** | Actively working through the application process | Yellow |
| **Client** | Signed and onboarded | Green |

## Funnel Visualization

At the top of the pipeline view, a visual bar chart shows the count of people at each stage with proportional bar heights. This gives you an instant sense of funnel shape — a healthy pipeline typically has more leads than applicants and more applicants than clients.

## Metrics Grid

Four cards show key pipeline health indicators:

| Metric | What It Measures |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Lead > Applicant rate** | Percentage of leads that advance to applicant |
| **Applicant > Client rate** | Percentage of applicants that become clients |
| **Avg Days as Lead** | How long people typically stay in the lead stage |
| **Velocity** | Stage transitions per week — how fast your pipeline moves |

## Conversion Window

Use the segmented control to view metrics over different periods: **30, 60, 90, or 180 days**. Shorter windows show recent momentum; longer windows show trends.

## Needs Attention

When someone has been stuck at a stage longer than expected, they appear in the orange **"Needs Attention"** section with their name, current stage, and how long they've been stuck.

**Click any stuck contact** to navigate directly to their detail view, where you'll find SAM's specific coaching recommendations for moving them forward.

## Recent Transitions

A timeline of recent stage movements shows who advanced (or exited) and when:

- "Sarah Chen: Lead > Applicant — 2d ago"
- "Mike Torres: Applicant > Client — 5d ago"

Click any transition to navigate to that person's profile.

## Tips

- Check the "Needs Attention" section weekly — these are the people most likely to stall out
- A high Lead > Applicant rate with a low Applicant > Client rate suggests your qualification is good but your close process needs work
- Use the 30-day window to measure recent changes; use 90+ days for a reliable baseline
