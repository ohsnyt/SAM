# Your Identity and Signature Settings

## Setting Up Your Name

SAM uses your name when drafting invitations, follow-ups, and other communications. To make sure it's correct:

1. Open **SAM > Settings** (or press Command + comma)
2. Go to the **General** tab
3. Find the **Your Identity** section
4. Enter your **First Name** and **Last Name**
5. Set your **Default Closing** (e.g., "Best," or "Warm regards," or "Yours,")

### Auto-Fill from Contacts

If you haven't set your name yet, SAM will try to auto-fill it from your "Me" contact in Apple Contacts when you first open Settings. You can also click the **Auto-fill from Me Contact** button anytime to pull your name from Contacts.

## How SAM Uses Your Name

SAM adapts how it signs your messages based on your relationship with each person:

| Relationship | How SAM Signs | Example |
|-------------|---------------|---------|
| **Warm** (3+ interactions in the last 90 days) | First name only | "Best, Sarah" |
| **Formal** (newer or less frequent contact) | Full name | "Best, Sarah Mitchell" |

This happens automatically — you don't need to think about it.

## Signature Learning

Here's something clever: **SAM learns your preferred closing style.**

1. SAM drafts an invitation ending with "Best, Sarah"
2. Before sending, you edit the closing to "Warm regards, Sarah"
3. SAM notices you changed "Best," to "Warm regards,"
4. Next time SAM drafts a similar message, it uses "Warm regards,"

SAM tracks this separately for:
- **Warm relationships** vs. **formal relationships**
- **Different message types** (invitations, follow-ups, etc.)

Over time, SAM learns exactly how you like to sign different types of messages to different types of people.
