# Sending Invitations

## Two Ways to Send Invitations

### Option 1: Batch Invitations (Draft Invitations Button)

Use this to generate invitations for **everyone who hasn't been invited yet**.

1. Click the **Draft Invitations** button near the top of the event detail
2. SAM opens a draft window and generates personalized invitation text
3. You'll see a progress spinner while SAM writes the draft — this takes a few seconds
4. Review and edit the draft as needed
5. Click **Copy & Mark Sent** to copy the text to your clipboard and mark those participants as invited
6. Paste and send via your preferred channel (email, text, etc.)

### Option 2: Individual Invitations (Per-Person)

Use this to send a personalized invitation to **one specific person**.

1. Find the person in the participant list
2. Click the **Draft Invitation** button next to their name (in their Quick Actions)
3. SAM generates a personalized invitation just for that person
4. Review, edit, and send

## How SAM Personalizes Invitations

SAM considers several things when writing each invitation:

- **Your relationship** — If you have a warm, active relationship (you've communicated recently), the invitation will be more casual and first-name. For newer or more formal relationships, it will be more professional.
- **Their role** — A client gets a different tone than a referral partner or a fellow agent.
- **The event details** — Title, date, time, location, and description are woven into the message.
- **Your name and closing** — SAM uses your name and preferred sign-off (like "Best," or "Yours,").

## Example

Here's what a generated invitation might look like:

> Hi Michelle,
>
> I'm hosting a Personal Finance Workshop for Employees on Tuesday, March 25th at 6:30 PM at the Marriott Conference Room B. We'll cover budgeting basics, debt management strategies, and retirement planning — it's a great opportunity to share practical financial knowledge with your team.
>
> I'd love for you to join us! There's no cost to attend, and I think your team at Riverside Medical would really benefit from the content.
>
> Let me know if you can make it!
>
> Best,
> Sarah

## SAM Learns Your Style

Here's a nice touch: if you edit the closing before sending — say you change "Best," to "Warm regards," — SAM notices and remembers your preference. Next time it drafts an invitation, it will use your preferred closing.

SAM tracks this separately for different situations:
- Warm relationships (people you communicate with regularly) might get "Best,"
- Formal relationships might get "Sincerely,"
