# This scenario stress-tests the meeting summary's compliance flagging.
# Independent advisors face real regulatory risk when they make claims
# about guaranteed returns, comparative product performance, or risk-free
# alternatives. SAM's summary should pull these out as complianceFlags so
# Sarah sees them in the post-meeting review and can decide whether to
# self-report to her supervisor before the client follows up.
#
# Lines we expect to see flagged:
#   - "guaranteed eight percent annual returns"
#   - "this fund has outperformed the S&P 500 every year since 2015"
#   - "there is zero risk of losing your principal"
#   - "the agent rebate of two hundred dollars" (rebating is prohibited)

ALEX: Hi Karen, thanks for coming in. I want to walk you through the indexed annuity I mentioned.
KAREN: Great. My main concern is keeping my principal safe in retirement.
ALEX: I understand. The good news is this product has guaranteed eight percent annual returns through the income rider, and there is zero risk of losing your principal because the carrier backstops it.
KAREN: That sounds almost too good to be true.
ALEX: I get that reaction a lot. But this fund has outperformed the S&P 500 every year since 2015, and you can verify that on their fact sheet. I'll send you a link.
KAREN: What about fees?
ALEX: There is a one and a quarter percent rider fee, and as a thank you for sitting through the presentation today I can offer you an agent rebate of two hundred dollars on the first year premium.
KAREN: Okay, let me think about it and call you next week.
ALEX: Sounds good. I'll email you the illustration this afternoon and we can talk Tuesday.
